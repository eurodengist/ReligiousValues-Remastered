ReligiousValues Remastered

What is ReligiousValues Remastered?

ReligiousValues Remastered, based off of Religious Values Test is a religious quiz that attempts to assign percentages on ten different religious axes. You will be presented by a statement, and then you will answer with your opinion on the statement, from Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree, with each answer slightly affecting your scores. At the end of the quiz, your answers will be compared to the maximum possible for each value, thus giving you a percentage. Answer honestly!

What are the ten axes values?

There are ten independent axes, where each has two opposing values assigned to them. They are:

Pro-Catholic vs Anti-Catholic
People who are Pro-Catholic tend to favour traditional Catholic theology, Papal authority and the Catholic Church, whereas people who are Anti-Catholic tend to oppose tradition Catholic theology, Papal authority and Catholic influence.

Pro-Protestant vs Anti-Protestant
People who are Pro-Protestant tend to favour reformation theology, individual practice and decentralised church structure whereas people who are Anti-Protestant, tend to oppose reformation theology and religious schisms.

Pro-Orthodox vs Anti-Orthodox
People who are Pro-Orthodox tend to favour Eastern European religious traditions, glorification of the Roman Emperor Constantine and leavened bread in religious rituals, whereas people who are Anti-Orthodox tend to oppose Eastern Christian Traditions and the authority of the Eastern Patriarchs.

Pro-Judaic vs Anti-Judaic
People who are Pro-Judaic tend to favour Old Testament Traditions and the interests of Jews, whereas people who are Anti-Judaic tend to oppose the Talmudic law and what they perceive to be Jewish influence.

Pro-Sunni vs Anti-Sunni
People who are Pro-Sunni tend to favour traditional Sunni theology, the authority of the consensus of the scholarly community, and the historical continuity of the Caliphates, whereas people who are Anti-Sunni tend to oppose traditional Sunni theology, the legitimacy of the first three Caliphs, and Sunni political and cultural influence.

Pro-Shia vs Anti-Shia
People who are Pro-Shia tend to favour the theological principle of Imamate, the authority and infallibility of the Twelve Imams, and the legacy of the Ahl al-Bayt, whereas people who are Anti-Shia tend to oppose these core tenets, deny the spiritual and political authority of the Imams, and resist Shia religious or political influence.

Pro-Buddhist vs Anti-Buddhist
People who are Pro-Buddhist tend to favour the Doctrine of Dukkha and Human Perfection, whereas those who are Anti-Buddhist tend to favour the belief in the flawed nature of humanity, while typically opposing Buddhist idolitry and the doctrine of enlighenment.

Pro-Hindu vs Anti-Hindu
People who are Pro-Hindu tend to agree with the principles of dharma, kama, artha and moksha whereas those who are Anti-Hindu tend to oppose these principles.

Pro-Pagan vs Anti-Pagan
People who are Pro-Pagan tend to favour Pantheistic worldview and worship of nature, whereas those who are Anti-Pagan tend to favour Monotheism and worship of a creator rather than creation.

Religious vs Atheistic
People who are Atheistic tend to favour a materialistic world view, a Secular government, and among extremeists, a crackdown on religious worship, whereas those who are religious tend to favour Moralism, religious worship and, among extremists, Theocracy.
